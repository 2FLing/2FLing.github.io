Soft Skills: the software developer's life manual

There is a book called book called Soft Skills: the software developer's life manual. This book was written by a software developer in the United States, but there is no one line of code in the book except for the words Java and C, because this book is about things other than code.Let me summarize the main points for you.
  • Don't be an otaku.
  • make a friend with the interviewer and then go to interview
  • How to become a freelancer?
  • Pretend yourself is successful.
  • Build your own brand: stick to blogging.
  • Effective management of time to enhance efficiency.
  • Learn to manage money: be good at stock speculation.
  • Don't blow up credit cards.
  • Watch less TV, exercise more, and try to become muscle men.

Today, many people believe that software developers are fat men with a beard, a dirty white T-shirt and a pizza. Now most software developers are directly proportional to the size of their stomach and age, which is not a good sign.
